Other Physics Fields

Beyond my current focus on High Energy Physics, my academic background includes extensive research in materials science, microscopy, and computational modeling.

Biophysics and Microscopy

My work in this area focuses on the intersection of biological systems and advanced imaging techniques. By leveraging high-resolution microscopy, we can probe the fundamental physical properties of biological structures.

  • Advanced Imaging: Experience with electron microscopy and optical techniques to study cellular dynamics.
  • Instrumentation: Developing and calibrating sensitive measurement tools for biological applications.

Computational Materials Science

Building on my Master's degree from Arizona State University, I utilize computational tools to predict and analyze the behavior of materials at the atomic and molecular scales.

  • Molecular Dynamics: Simulating material properties under various thermodynamic conditions.
  • DFT Calculations: Applying Density Functional Theory to study the electronic structure of novel materials.
  • Data Analysis: Utilizing Python and high-performance computing (HPC) environments to process large-scale simulation data.
