High Energy Physics Research

My current research focuses on experimental neutrino physics, contributing to the next generation of deep underground detectors designed to unravel the mysteries of the universe.

DUNE (Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment)

As a PhD student at Indiana University, I am a member of the DUNE collaboration. This international flagship experiment aims to study neutrino oscillations, search for proton decay, and observe neutrinos from supernovae to understand the matter-antimatter asymmetry in the universe.

  • Collaboration: Working under the supervision of Prof. Jon Urheim within the DUNE Photon Detection System (PDS) consortium.
  • Objective: Enhancing the energy resolution and timing of neutrino interaction events using scintillation light.

ProtoDUNE Vertical Drift (VD)

My primary technical focus is on the Photon Detection System (PDS) for the ProtoDUNE Vertical Drift detector. This large-scale prototype at CERN validates the technology for the full-scale DUNE Far Detector modules.

  • Hardware Integration: Involved in the development and testing of photon collectors (such as X-ARAPUCAs) for detecting 128nm scintillation light in Liquid Argon (LAr).
  • Vertical Drift Technology: Studying the unique challenges of placing PDS components on the cathode and cryostat membranes in a high-voltage environment.
  • Data Analysis: Analyzing detector performance and light yield to optimize the reconstruction of neutrino events.
